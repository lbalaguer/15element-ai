Frequently asked

B2B LinkedIn prospecting with buying signals.

What are buying signals in B2B LinkedIn prospecting?
Buying signals are observable changes that mean a company is more likely to need what you sell right now: a new decision-maker in a relevant role, an expansion or new location, active hiring on a team you serve, funding, or a public initiative. Instead of blasting a static list, we monitor for these signals and reach out the moment the context is right — to the person who has the problem today.
How is this different from mass LinkedIn outreach or Sales Navigator spam?
Mass outreach sends the same template to thousands and hopes. We start from a real signal, qualify each company against your ICP, map the actual decision-maker, and write the message around their specific context — so reply rates run far higher and only qualified prospects with decision authority reach your sales team. Quality over volume: in B2B the right ten beat ten thousand wrong ones.
Will this get my LinkedIn account restricted?
No. Because we work from signals and qualify tightly, volumes stay within safe, human limits and messages are relevant rather than spammy — which is exactly what keeps accounts healthy. We're not running a blast tool; we're running targeted, contextual outreach that looks like what it is: a real person reaching out with a reason.
How fast do qualified calls show up?
Typically the first qualified conversations appear within weeks 3-6 of an active campaign. A Toronto Royal LePage broker saw +310% qualified consultations; Mercedes-Benz closed three $50K Sprinters from a single LinkedIn message. Timing depends on your ticket, sales cycle and how active your buying signals are — we set an honest expectation in the diagnosis.
