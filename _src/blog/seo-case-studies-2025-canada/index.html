Case 1 — Propel Active Health & Lifestyle (Sports Chiropractic, Burlington)

Category: Sports Chiropractic & Rehabilitation · Location: Burlington, Ontario · Website: propelactive.com

Propel Active is a sports chiropractic clinic competing in one of the most crowded healthcare categories in the GTA — local rehab and chiropractic. The challenge wasn't getting found at all; it was standing out among dozens of clinics within a 10 km radius all chasing the same buyer.

The play was a content architecture built around the questions a Burlington patient actually asks before they book: knee pain relief treatments in Burlington, best ergonomic cervical pillow, signs your child may benefit from paediatric rehabilitation, common cycling injuries prevention guide. Pages built for the search, not for the brochure. Add structured services (rehabilitation, medical acupuncture, pregnancy care, custom-fit orthotics) with Schema.org markup, and the entity becomes legible to both Google and the AI engines.

Results over 8 months:

  • Clicks: 289 → 411 (+42%)
  • Impressions: 20,443 → 22,001 (+8%)
  • Leads: 557 → 617 (+11%)
  • SERP position: Top 3 Canadian results for core category queries
  • AI Overviews: now cited in Google's AI answers for several Burlington rehab queries
  • Acquisition cost: −55% measured against the previous period

This is what compounding looks like on an established site. The base was already there — the work was making each page do its job for a specific question, then linking the architecture so Google understands the clinic's authority in the category.

Case 2 — Priority One Osteopathy & Wellness (Manual Osteopathy, Burlington)

Category: Manual Osteopathy · Location: Burlington, Ontario · Website: priorityoneosteoandwellness.com

Priority One started near zero. A small osteopathy practice competing against larger, more established wellness providers in Burlington — the kind of business that "doesn't believe in SEO" because the previous experience didn't work. The opposite of Propel: low starting authority, niche category, owner-operator with no marketing team.

The advantage of starting near zero in a clear niche is that the compounding curve is steeper. Each piece of structured content adds disproportionate authority because there's nothing in the way. The play was direct: a clean page architecture explaining manual osteopathy in language a real patient uses, location-specific service pages, and FAQ content marked with FAQPage schema that AI engines can quote verbatim.

Results over 8 months:

  • Clicks: 21 → 75 (+257%)
  • Impressions: 519 → 1,733 (+234%)
  • Leads: 34 → 109 (+221%)
  • SERP position: top results for "osteopathy Burlington" and adjacent queries
  • AI Overviews: now cited for manual osteopathy queries in Burlington

If anyone tells you SEO doesn't work for a small Canadian practice in a competitive city, this is the counter-example. The work isn't fancy — it's consistent, structured, and aimed at the buyer's actual question.

Case 3 — MCS Electrical Solutions (Certified Electricians, Burlington)

Category: Certified Electricians (Residential & Commercial) · Location: Burlington, Ontario · Website: mcselectrical.ca

The skilled trades category in Burlington is dense — dozens of certified electricians with overlapping service areas, similar pricing, near-identical websites, and most of them buying Google Ads. MCS Electrical's challenge was being chosen before the price comparison even started. The lever was authority signals a buyer can verify in three seconds: certifications, 24/7 availability, review velocity, and Map Pack visibility.

The technical play covered the basics most trades sites get wrong: schema for LocalBusiness with proper NAP consistency across directories, service-area pages that answer the buyer's first question ("do you cover my neighbourhood?"), and content that demonstrates the work — not stock photos of someone in a hard hat. Layer in consistent Google Business Profile activity, review-velocity discipline (130+ reviews at 5.0★), and citation hygiene across Canadian trades directories, and the entity becomes the obvious choice in the Map Pack.

Results over 7 months:

  • Clicks: 24 → 53 (+121%)
  • Impressions: 2,818 → 5,664 (+101%)
  • Leads: 37 → 57 (+54%)
  • SERP position: "electrician Burlington" moved from position 23 → Top 10
  • Google Map Pack: Top 3 for Burlington electrician searches
  • ChatGPT: ranked Top 5 in ChatGPT answers for "electrician Burlington"
  • Reputation: 5.0★ rating with 130+ reviews

This is the case study to read if you run a trades business and you're tired of bidding on the same ads as everyone else. The Map Pack is owned by the businesses that earn it — and once you earn it, the cost of acquisition drops because the buyer already chose you before the call.

Case 4 — Oakville Sight & Sound (Smart Home & Home Theatre, Oakville)

Category: Luxury Smart Home Automation & Home Theatre · Location: Oakville, Ontario · Website: oakvillesightandsound.ca

Oakville Sight & Sound competes against national brands with seven-figure marketing budgets in a category where the buyer expects bespoke work — luxury smart home installation, home theatre design, custom integration. The challenge wasn't volume; it was being the obvious local choice for a buyer who could afford to call anyone.

The play was specificity. Pages built around the work — not generic "smart home" copy — paired with portfolio content that demonstrates installations, schema markup that makes Google understand the service-area scope, and an authority architecture that positions the business as the Oakville specialist (not "an Ontario installer"). When a high-intent buyer searches in Oakville, the local specialist beats the national brand every time — provided Google can tell who the local specialist is.

Results over 9 months:

  • Clicks: 142 → 232 (+63%)
  • Impressions: 20,168 → 31,613 (+57%)
  • Leads: 6 → 17 (+183%)
  • SERP position: #1 for "home automation Oakville" and adjacent queries

The lead number matters more than the click number here. In luxury home automation, a single project can be six figures. Going from 6 to 17 qualified leads in 9 months is the difference between a business owner spending the year worried and a business owner with a planning problem.

Results across all four — what they have in common

Different categories, different cities, different starting authority. The pattern is the same.

  • 100% organic. No ad spend in any of the four. Every click, every lead came from search.
  • Timeframe 7–9 months. Compounding starts in month 3, accelerates from there. Anyone selling 30-day SEO results in a real category is selling something else.
  • Lead growth outpaced click growth in 3 of 4. Better ranking on the right query converts more, not just brings more traffic. Priority One and Oakville Sight & Sound are the clearest examples.
  • AEO citations are real. Two of the four are cited in Google AI Overviews; one ranks Top 5 in ChatGPT for its core category query. This is where the category is going — most Canadian local competitors haven't noticed yet.
  • Local-first architecture beats national-first copy. Every business won by being specifically Burlington, specifically Oakville — not by trying to look like a national brand.

What makes an SEO case study credible in 2026?

How long does a Canadian local business take to see SEO results?

First measurable movement in GSC: weeks 6–12. Meaningful business outcomes (leads, bookings): months 4–9 depending on category competition and starting authority. Priority One moved fastest on a low base; Propel Active moved on an already-established site.

Do these results require Google Ads spend?

No. All four cases are 100% organic. Ads are a separate decision and don't compound the way organic authority does.
